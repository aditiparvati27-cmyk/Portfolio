01 — Context

How I ended up
in this space.

Six months ago, I was working on a project for Capital One. The question: if someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity "what's the best credit card for travel rewards," does Capital One show up?

It often didn't. We started evaluating AI brand visibility platforms to understand why. That's how I found this category.

I spent the next several months going deep. Mapped the competitive landscape, studied the protocol stack being built underneath it, tracked how the category was shifting from monitoring toward something more structural: how AI agents actually interact with brands.

This brief is a slice of that research.

The original question

"When a consumer asks an AI engine for product recommendations in our category, are we appearing? If not, why? And what would we need to change?"

What it turned into

A six-month research sprint across the full AEO/GEO landscape: platform evaluation, protocol analysis (MCP, ACP, UCP, WebMCP, A2A), competitive mapping, and a product strategy brief for one of the companies in the space.

05 — The Full Funnel

AEO only covers
the first layer.

The AI-to-brand relationship has three layers. Most platforms stop at layer one.

LAYER 01 — DISCOVERY
AI sees you
AI mentions your brand. Citation tracking, visibility scores, share of voice. This is where most AEO platforms live today.
COMMODITIZING
LAYER 02 — INTERACTION
AI visits you
An agent navigates to your site, tries to understand your product catalog. CDN-level crawler tracking, bot identification.
EMERGING
LAYER 03 — TRANSACTION
AI buys for you
Agent completes a purchase. Search, add to cart, checkout. Did it succeed? Fail? Redirect to a competitor? This is where revenue flows. No platform fully owns it yet.
WIDE OPEN
06 — Why Scrunch

CDN-layer optimization is
the right technical bet.

I've been tracking this space closely. Scrunch stands out for specific, structural reasons.

What I see in Scrunch

→ AXP is the right technical direction. Serving structured, AI-optimized content at the CDN layer is the same concept as WebMCP but from the brand's infrastructure side. No competitor is building at this layer.
→ 98% file size reduction for AI consumption is a real engineering achievement. This directly maps to the token cost problem — cheaper for AI models to process means more interactions.
→ SOC 2 Type II is done. Enterprise procurement cares about this. Some competitors with 5x the funding haven't completed it.
→ 500+ brands and 50%+ MoM growth before the Series A shows genuine product-market fit on the monitoring side.
→ The CDN approach means zero disruption. Brands optimize for AI agents without changing their human-facing website. That's a clean deployment story for enterprise.

Where the opportunity is

→ The insights gap is real and fixable. Users consistently say monitoring is solid but the "what to do about it" layer is underdeveloped. Closing that gap turns data into action.
→ AXP's go-to-market challenge is explaining CDN-layer agent optimization to marketing buyers who think in terms of content and keywords. That's a GTM problem, not an engineering problem.
→ WebMCP integration is a natural extension. AXP already operates at the same layer where WebMCP tool registrations will live. The infrastructure overlap is significant.
→ The commerce protocol stack (ACP, UCP) needs CDN-layer support. Scrunch's existing infrastructure is the right foundation for delivering agent-optimized commerce data.
