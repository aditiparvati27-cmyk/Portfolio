01 — Context

How I ended up
in this space.

Six months ago, I was working on a project for Capital One. The question: if someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity "what's the best credit card for travel rewards," does Capital One show up?

It often didn't. We started evaluating AI brand visibility platforms to understand why. That's how I found this category.

I spent the next several months going deep. Mapped the competitive landscape, studied the protocol stack being built underneath it, tracked how the category was shifting from monitoring toward something more structural: how AI agents actually interact with brands.

This brief is a slice of that research.

The original question

"When a consumer asks an AI engine for product recommendations in our category, are we appearing? If not, why? And what would we need to change?"

What it turned into

A six-month research sprint across the full AEO/GEO landscape: platform evaluation, protocol analysis (MCP, ACP, UCP, WebMCP, A2A), competitive mapping, and a product strategy brief for one of the companies in the space.

05 — The Full Funnel

AEO only covers
the first layer.

The AI-to-brand relationship has three layers. Most platforms stop at layer one.

LAYER 01 — DISCOVERY
AI sees you
AI mentions your brand. Citation tracking, visibility scores, share of voice. This is where most AEO platforms live today.
COMMODITIZING
LAYER 02 — INTERACTION
AI visits you
An agent navigates to your site, tries to understand your product catalog. CDN-level crawler tracking, bot identification.
EMERGING
LAYER 03 — TRANSACTION
AI buys for you
Agent completes a purchase. Search, add to cart, checkout. Did it succeed? Fail? Redirect to a competitor? This is where revenue flows. No platform fully owns it yet.
WIDE OPEN
06 — Why Gauge

Agent analytics is the
right framing.

Most platforms in this space are monitoring dashboards. Gauge is explicitly building toward action-oriented workflows. That's the gap.

What I see in Gauge

→ "Generates optimization actions" is the right positioning. The gap between monitoring (what happened) and optimization (what to do about it) is where most platforms lose customers. Gauge is building to close it.
→ Agent analytics framing is forward-looking. While others focus on citation monitoring, Gauge is positioning around how agents behave — which is where the entire market is headed.
→ Enterprise-only pricing signals real client relationships. No self-serve freemium dilution. Selling into organizations, not individual marketers.
→ Publishing competitive analysis (AEO Improvement Score) is a smart category-defining move. It puts Gauge in the conversation alongside better-funded companies.

Where the opportunity is

→ The agent analytics → agent transactions extension. Closing the attribution loop from "AI mentioned your brand" to "AI completed a transaction on your site" — that's the $15T opportunity.
→ Protocol integration (ACP, UCP, WebMCP) would give Gauge the data layer to track not just agent citations but agent commerce activity. No platform has this yet.
→ The action-oriented workflow approach maps directly to how enterprise buyers think: "don't just tell me the problem, help me fix it." That's what separates tools that get renewed from tools that get cut.
→ If Gauge is early-stage, one person with deep category knowledge can shape both product direction and the GTM narrative. That's the window.
