01 — Context

How I ended up
in this space.

Six months ago, I was working on a project for Capital One. The question was straightforward: if someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity "what's the best credit card for travel rewards," does Capital One show up?

It often didn't. We started evaluating AI brand visibility platforms to understand why. That's how I found this category.

I spent the next several months going deep. Not surface-level. I mapped the competitive landscape, studied the protocol stack being built underneath it, and started tracking how the category was shifting from monitoring toward something more structural: how AI agents actually interact with brands.

This brief is a slice of that research. It's what I'd want to know if I were building in this space.

The original question

"When a consumer asks an AI engine for product recommendations in our category, are we appearing? If not, why? And what would we need to change?"

05 — The Full Funnel

AEO only covers
the first layer.

The AI-to-brand relationship has three layers. Most platforms stop at layer one.

LAYER 01 — DISCOVERY
AI sees you
AI mentions your brand in a response. This is where AEO platforms live today. Citation tracking, visibility scores, share of voice. Bluefish's AI Monitoring module does this well — custom prompts, Custom AI Audiences, AI Brand Vault.
COMMODITIZING
LAYER 02 — INTERACTION
AI visits you
An agent navigates to your site, tries to understand your product catalog, reads your content. CDN-level crawler tracking, bot identification, page depth analysis. Bluefish's Agentic Commerce module is entering this space.
EMERGING
LAYER 03 — TRANSACTION
AI buys for you
An agent completes a purchase on behalf of a user. Search products, add to cart, checkout. Did it succeed? Fail? Redirect to a competitor? This is where revenue actually flows. No platform fully owns it yet.
WIDE OPEN
The brand that helps enterprises go from "we know AI mentions us" to "we know AI can transact with us, and here's how to fix it when it can't" — that brand defines the next category.

How Bluefish compares
to the field.

Fortune 500 focus (80%+ clients). Custom AI Audiences, AI Brand Vault, source influence mapping. Strength: founding team + enterprise relationships. Gap: no content creation tools, no protocol integrations yet.
PROFOUND — $155M

CDN-Layer Agent Optimization

Ex-Google Search / DeepMind founders. Direct Shopify revenue attribution. Strength: can tie AI visibility to dollars. Gap: small team (17), no agentic commerce layer.
