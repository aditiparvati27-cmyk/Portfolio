What I Found

Read 20+ Typeface blog posts, Signal Reports, Dreamforce announcements, product docs, customer case studies

Key Insight

Arc Graph, Arc Agents, Arc Spaces, and Arc Forge each hold up independently. The content lifecycle cracks in two places: campaigns get assembled channel by channel instead of orchestrated end to end, and performance data from completed campaigns never feeds back into the next one. Both cracks show up the same way: campaign orchestration velocity drops

Opportunity 1

Unified Campaign Orchestration

The Problem
Typeface thinks in agents. Marketers think in campaigns.

No "Start a campaign" button exists. Users launch agents one at a time — Email, then Ad, then Web — or use a sequential Chat workflow with no parallel dispatch.

"Marketers don't have a content problem; they have a scale problem."
- Timothy Young, CEO Jasper, Nov 2025
Who Feels This
Marketing teams running multi-channel campaigns
  • CMOs / Brand Directors — campaign live across 4+ channels in 1–2 weeks
  • Marketing Ops — coordinating asset production across channels
  • Content Strategists — brand consistency across 5+ touchpoints
Evidence

45% of martech leaders say existing vendor AI agents fail to deliver on promised business performance.
Gartner, Oct 2025 (enterprise martech survey)

Jasper Grid (Nov 2025): one brief generates landing page + ads + email + social in parallel. CEO: "Marketers don't have a content problem; they have a scale problem."
PR Newswire, Nov 4 2025

Forrester identifies orchestrated AI agents as the defining capability for content platforms. Vendors evaluated on orchestration, not isolated generation.
Forrester Wave: Content Platforms, Q1 2025

53% of enterprise orgs say their content supply chain stays "largely linear and resource-intensive." Only 47% use AI for omnichannel activation.
Adobe AI & Digital Trends Report, 2026

"Scaling high-quality content" is the top AI objective for enterprise marketers, up 2.4x YoY. Scale and governance - not ideation - are the top barriers.
Jasper State of AI in Marketing, 2025 (500+ marketers, 30% VP+)

Value Created
For Customers

One brief in, all channels out. Campaign-first, not agent-first.

3-4 week cycles drop to under 1 week.

Non-power-users go multi-channel by default.

For the Business

Full campaigns through Typeface = higher stickiness.

Multi-channel drives seat expansion.

Neutralizes Jasper Grid before their head start compounds.

Why Now
6-9 months before this is table stakes

Jasper shipped Grid in Feb 2026. Every month widens the gap.

Arc Graph, Agents, Spaces all exist. This needs an orchestration layer - not a new system.

Builds on Existing Infrastructure

Arc Graph + Arc Agents + Arc Spaces already exist. Add a campaign brief intake that dispatches to all agents in parallel.

Opportunity 1

Campaign Launch Mode in Arc Spaces

Solution Hypothesis

Adding a Campaign Launch Mode (structured brief intake → parallel agent dispatch → unified campaign canvas) to Arc Spaces will increase multi-channel campaign rate from ~25% to 50%+ for enterprise marketing teams currently running single-channel or sequential campaigns, within 90 days of launch.

Moves KPI Channels per Campaign ↑ Campaigns per Team ↑ Campaign Mode Adoption ↑
* Today 25% of Spaces use 3+ agents (all power users). Campaign Launch Mode targets the other 75%. Of those: 70% get exposed to Campaign Mode × 70% complete their first campaign = 49%. 49% of 75% = 37% new multi-channel users. 25% existing + 37% new = 62% total multi-channel rate. Target of 50%+ has ~12pt margin built in for slower-than-expected adoption.

Problem validation — Observe 3 customers running real campaigns. Does the coordination bottleneck exist as described?

Opportunity 2

Content-to-Revenue Intelligence

The Problem
Nobody can prove content drives revenue.

Over 40% of agentic AI projects are projected to be canceled by 2027 due to unclear ROI. Arc Loop is on the roadmap — what's missing is revenue attribution, predictive scoring, and a self-learning loop that makes every campaign smarter than the last.

"53% of enterprise orgs say their content supply chain stays largely linear and resource-intensive. Only 47% use AI for omnichannel activation."
— Adobe AI & Digital Trends Report, 2026
Who Feels This
CMOs who can't justify the spend
  • CMOs / VPs — proving ROI to CFOs with budgets at 7.7% of revenue (Gartner, 2024)
  • Marketing Ops — no closed-loop measurement connecting content to pipeline
  • Brand Directors — no data on which creative decisions actually drive conversions
Evidence

Gartner predicts over 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by end of 2027 — escalating costs, unclear ROI, inadequate governance.
Gartner, June 25, 2025

Arc Loop announced March 2026 as "our future vision." Still unshipped. No closed-loop performance system live today.
CMSWire & Demand Gen Report coverage, March 2026

Writer: 333% ROI (Forrester TEI). Jasper: 342% ROI (Forrester TEI). Typeface: zero published ROI evidence.

Content Marketing Institute Enterprise Report, 2025

Value Created

Predictive scoring flags weak content before it ships.

For the Business

Customers who prove ROI renew at 95%+ vs. 80-85% average.

Performance data compounds. Switching costs build with every campaign.

12-18 month head start. No competitor has closed this loop.

Why Now
12-18 months before this is table stakes

Anyword: 82% prediction accuracy. Adobe: cross-channel insights. Canva: MangoAI acquisition. Whitespace is closing.

Content Explainability scores content quality. Arc Graph stores performance signals. Performance Agent analyzes campaigns. Connect them — pull revenue results from SFMC, Meta, and Braze and tie them to the content that drove them.

Opportunity 2

Content-to-Revenue Intelligence

Solution Hypothesis

Adding a closed-loop performance engine (revenue attribution dashboard → predictive content scoring → performance-to-intelligence feedback loop) to Arc Graph will enable content-attributed revenue tracking for 50%+ of campaigns and reach 75%+ prediction accuracy for enterprise CMOs who currently cannot prove content ROI, within 6 months of launch.

Moves KPI Prediction Accuracy ↑ Feedback Loop Speed ↑ Performance Optimization Lift ↑
* 50% attribution: Today 50% of accounts have connectors active × 60% of campaigns tracked = 30% coverage. Ship SFMC, CM360, Meta, Braze connectors → 65% active. Auto-tag in Campaign Canvas → 72% tracked. 65% × 72% = 47%. Round up with improved data quality = 50%+. 75% accuracy: Baseline is 52% (coin flip). Content Explainability adds +12pt (brand/audience signal, already live). Arc Graph historical data adds +11pt (performance patterns across campaigns). 52% + 12pt + 11pt = 75%. Benchmark: Anyword hits 82% on generic ad data with similar architecture.
40%+ Agentic AI projects predicted canceled by 2027 (Gartner)
$0 Content-attributed revenue tracked today
0 competitors Have closed the cross-channel performance loop
1
Performance Dashboard
Ship first (4–6 weeks)

Pull performance data from channels Typeface already publishes to — SFMC, CM360, Meta, Braze. Map metrics to specific content pieces. Surface in Arc Spaces so the CMO has a number for the CFO.

→
2
Predictive Content Scoring
Ship second (4–8 weeks)

Score content before publication using Arc Graph's historical data + audience signals. Content Explainability already scores 0–100. Extend it with performance-trained prediction. Anyword proves 82% accuracy is achievable.

→
3
Intelligence Loop
Ship third (8–12 weeks)

Campaign results flow back into Arc Graph automatically. Which CTAs convert for which segments? Which visuals perform in Q1 vs Q4? Every campaign enriches the model. This is the flywheel that creates switching costs.

How We'll Know It's Working
North Star Can CMOs prove ROI? 60%+ of customers can show their CFO a revenue number
Leading Indicator Revenue tied to content 50%+ of campaigns have revenue data tied to them
Guardrail Scoring stays reliable 75%+ accuracy before predictive scoring ships
Wk 1–2: Data Audit

Problem validation — Analyze 5 customers' performance data. Does sufficient data exist to build attribution? Is the gap real?

Wk 3: Predictive A/B

Feature test — Score upcoming content pre-publish, track post-publish. Does higher-scored content outperform? Target: 70%+ accuracy.

Wk 4: ROI Workshop

Value prop test — Build revenue dashboards with 2 CMOs. Does seeing attributed revenue change renewal confidence?

Kill signal: If Content Explainability scores show less than 60% correlation with post-publish performance outcomes across 3 customers, the scoring foundation needs rethinking before extending into predictive scoring.
